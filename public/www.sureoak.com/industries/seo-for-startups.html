Why is SEO important for startups?

Every business needs a website. In fact,  53% of shoppers research online before making a purchase, according to Google. And whether or not you sell directly through your website, your website needs to be visible and valuable to users if you want to win their trust and turn them into customers. There are two main ways to gain visibility and earn traffic to your website: organic marketing and paid marketing.

Organic traffic is earned naturally — i.e. without paying for higher placement in search results. This is accomplished by providing valuable and relevant content that aligns with users’ intentions. Paid search traffic is earned by running ads and paying for clicks, better known as pay-per-click (PPC) advertising.

While there’s value in leveraging both tactics to generate traffic, embracing organic marketing produces valuable results and traffic for the long-term without having to continuously pay for visibility and clicks.

Implementing a comprehensive content strategy for startups is the best way to establish authority — a crucial ranking factor — on search engines. And, in turn, build stronger relationships with consumers, as that authority will help drive home the fact your products and services are the best available.

Earning organic traffic through a content marketing strategy for startups takes time and dedication, but provides more value over time, as traffic from organic search grows exponentially as a page’s ranking increases in SERPs — i.e. making the jump from fourth to third will produce exponentially more clicks.

Local Business

Four out of five consumers use search engines to find businesses near them, making local SEO services essential to visibility for any small business just starting up in their community and nearby communities.

These may include:

  • Creating content that speaks directly to local audiences.

  • Name, address, and phone number (NAP) optimization.
